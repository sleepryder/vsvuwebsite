Methodology

Technological aspects, tools used, and process description.

Description

This project delves into a deceptively simple yet profound question: Why can we never truly see ourselves? The question, so seemingly trivial, lacks a satisfying answer, and it is within this absence that the essence of my work lies.

Using experimental methods, I explore self-manifestation, producing video recordings that act as projections of my identity. These projections are neither fixed nor complete; they hover in the liminal space of “becoming". Abstract and symbolic, they eschew finality, focusing instead on the continuous evolution of what it means to be human.

Exhibition Description

Becoming Over Being

The installation explores the paradox of self-awareness, using an enclosed box to isolate both the artist and the audience. This box, simultaneously a barrier and a lens, symbolizes the impossibility of fully perceiving oneself, or another, as they truly are.

Inside, fragmented video performances of self-projections unfold: abstract, symbolic acts that resist resolution, capturing the ongoing process of "becoming" rather than being.

Viewers remain on the outside, witnessing only glimpses of these acts. This separation blurs the line between presence and absence, reflecting the shared struggle to grasp an elusive truth.
